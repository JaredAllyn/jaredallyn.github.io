Bio

Add headshot.jpg to /images

Jared Allyn is currently serving as a Peace Corps Volunteer in Botswana, where he teaches English and leads digital literacy workshops for students and teachers. During his service, he re-founded the Botswana chapter of Democrats Abroad and currently chairs the country committee.

Before Botswana, Jared spent nearly a decade building and running an independent content platform on YouTube, starting in high school, growing it to over 420,000 subscribers and 80 million views and ranking in the top 0.2% of creators globally. Jared channeled the digital and communications expertise he developed through that work into supporting nonprofits and civic engagement organizations during his time at Cleveland State University, where he served as a Democracy Fellow with the Campus Vote Project and Media and Communications Coordinator for the Office of Civic Engagement. Jared holds a Bachelor of Arts in Linguistics from Cleveland State University and is an alumnus of the competitive Public Policy and International Affairs (PPIA) fellowship at the University of Michigan’s Ford School, where he completed graduate-level coursework in microeconomics, statistics, technology policy, and public management.

Jared’s experiences, from building an independent media platform to supporting voter engagement on campus to teaching digital literacy in southern Africa, have shaped a focused interest in social media policy, civic technology, and the role of digital infrastructure in democratic participation.
