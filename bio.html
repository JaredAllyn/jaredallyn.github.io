Bio

Add headshot.jpg to /images

Jared Allyn is interested in civic technology and policy communication, and he holds a Bachelor of Arts in Linguistics from Cleveland State University and completed a graduate-level fellowship at the Gerald R. Ford School of Public Policy at the University of Michigan through the PPIA program.

He currently serves as a Peace Corps Education Volunteer in Botswana, where he teaches English and digital literacy, builds teacher capacity, and organizes civic engagement efforts for Americans abroad through Democrats Abroad Botswana.

Before Peace Corps, Jared built a YouTube channel to over 420,000 subscribers and 80 million views over eight years — an experience that sharpened his instincts around how people consume information and what it takes to communicate clearly at scale. That work now informs his focus on using technology to make democracy more accessible.

He is interested in the intersection of AI, civic infrastructure, and public communication and is actively building tools aimed at lowering the barriers to democratic participation.
